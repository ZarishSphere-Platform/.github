# Zarish Sphere

## A Unified Digital Heartbeat for Humanitarian Impact

> The Zarish Sphere Platform is an enterprise-grade, distributed system designed to manage the full spectrum of operations for a global non-profit development organization. It serves as a unified digital ecosystem, replacing fragmented monolithic systems (like traditional ERP, EMR, EHR and HIS platforms) with a modern, modular, and flexible architecture. The platform is designed for maximum reusability, zero-touch provisioning, and robust offline capability, ensuring its utility from the central HQ down to remote field units.

## Vision

To be the global standard for modular, open-source, data-driven development and humanitarian operations management, empowering low-resource environments with high-reliability, offline-first technology, and fostering evidence-based research for maximum impact.

## Mission

To provide a secure, scalable, and customizable digital platform that unifies global administrative and clinical operations (ERP, EMR, EHR, and HIS), enabling real-time synchronization of data across all organizational tiers, from the field of execution to the organizational headquarters.

## Core Principles

*   **Code reuse and reduction of duplicated effort** - reusable components, APIs, and templates.
*   **Modular architecture** - allows for easy addition, removal, or updating of modules to extend functionality or tailor the system to specific needs.
*   **Configuration-first** - each frontend module can specify its config properties.
*   **Usability** - optimized UI for point of service, responsiveness, and arbitrarily complex patient care records.
*   **Enhanced UI** - more intuitive, user-friendly, and visually appealing UI.
*   **Modern technologies** - greater appeal to a global talent pool of developers.
*   **Improved interoperability** - support for FHIR and other standards.
*   **Central OAuth2 management system** for all levels.
*   Each and every module, service, and application will be pre-configured as **ready-to-use templates**, requiring minimal modification through click-based selection.
